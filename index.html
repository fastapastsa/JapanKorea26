Use Tabelog (not Google Maps) for restaurant research — Japan's local review platform. A score of 3.5+ means genuinely excellent. Avoid Roppongi and Shibuya main strip. Pivot to Ebisu, Nakameguro, Koenji, or Shimokitazawa for local pricing and real quality.

Gyeongbokgung Palace ₩3,000/pp (~A$3.30)
Seoul's most important cultural site. Changing of the guard every hour — time arrival for 2pm or 3pm. Walk uphill to Bukchon Hanok Village — traditional tile-roofed houses. Best in late afternoon when crowds thin.

Korean BBQ — local alleys ₩50,000–70,000/pp (~A$55–77)
Avoid tourist-facing Myeongdong strip. Back alleys behind Sinnonhyeon Station Exit 3 or the Mapo area. Look for full tables, smoke, and noise. Samgyeopsal (pork belly) or galbi (beef ribs), wrapped in lettuce with kimchi and garlic.
Korean BBQ order guide
Samgyeopsal (pork belly) grilled at table, wrapped in lettuce with ssamjang paste, garlic, kimchi. Galbi (beef short ribs) if splurging. Order doenjang jjigae (fermented soybean soup) on the side. Soju or OB beer.

South Korea · Days 8–9
Busan
5 – 7 October · 2 nights · KTX from Seoul
2 nights Seafood · Dwaeji Gukbap · Ssiat Hotteok Gamcheon Village · Jagalchi Market
Why Busan: Korea's coastal second city. Food culture distinct from Seoul — heavier on pork, seafood, and rice soups born from the Korean War refugee era. Far less tourist infrastructure. Best area to stay: Seomyeon (central, great food street) or Haeundae (beach).

Monday 5 October
KTX to Busan — Jagalchi Market, Gwangalli Beach
›
Morning
KTX Seoul → Busan ₩59,800/pp (~A$65)
Check out of Seoul hotel. Seoul Station. Store bags at Busan Station coin lockers or hotel if early check-in possible.
Afternoon
Jagalchi Seafood Market ~₩30,000–50,000/pp for seafood (~A$33–55)
Korea's largest seafood market. Pick live seafood from ground floor stalls — they take it upstairs and cook it for you. Try raw crab in soy sauce (gejang) — Busan's signature. Very authentic atmosphere.
Evening
Gwangalli Beach at night + bulgogi dinner ~₩25,000–35,000/pp (~A$27–38)
Famous lit bridge backdrop. Walk the beachfront, eat Eonyang-style bulgogi (minced beef patties, a Busan specialty) at beach restaurants. The neon + smoke + bridge at night is a highlight.
Must-eat in Busan
Dwaeji gukbap (pork rice soup ~₩8,000) — Seomyeon Food Street. Ssiat hotteok (seed-filled pancake ~₩2,000) — BIFF Square or Gukje Market. Eomuk (fish cake eaten with free hot broth ~₩1,000). Seafood at Jagalchi. Leegane Tteokbokki (at Gukje Market, ~₩3,000) — highly rated on Reddit.

Japan · Days 10–13
Fukuoka
7 – 11 October · 3 nights · Fly from Busan (~1 hr)
3 nights Tonkotsu Ramen · Yatai · Motsunabe · Mentaiko Dazaifu Shrine · Hakata Old Town Tenjin Underground · Canal City
Fukuoka is Japan's most underrated city. Birthplace of tonkotsu ramen. Best yatai (outdoor food stall) culture in Japan. Cheaper than Tokyo and Osaka. Fukuoka Airport is 5 minutes by subway from the city centre — the most convenient airport in Japan. Stay near Hakata or Tenjin Station.

Large underground mall directly connected to the subway. Japanese fashion at non-tourist prices. Walk the Daimyo neighbourhood above ground for independent boutiques.

The essential Fukuoka experience. Small open-air stalls along the river — tonkotsu ramen, yakitori, oden, mentaiko. Each stall seats 6–8 people. Split the group across two adjacent stalls. Go at 7pm when they open.

8:00 AM
Hakata ramen breakfast — Ippudo Daimyo or Shin-Shin ~¥900–1,200/pp (~A$10–13)
The only city in Japan where ramen at 8am is normal. Thin noodles, creamy pork bone broth, chashu, half egg. Order kaedama (extra noodles for ¥100) at the end.
10:00 AM
Dazaifu Tenmangu Shrine Free entry · Train ¥400/pp (~A$4.40) return
30 min from Hakata by train. One of Japan's most important Shinto shrines. The approach street has stalls selling umegae mochi (grilled rice cake ~¥150) — eat them fresh off the iron. Beautiful gardens and carp ponds.
3:00 PM
Hakata old town — Kushida Shrine & Machiya Museum Museum free · Shrine free
Genuinely un-touristy old Japan. Narrow streets, small temples, local life. The merchant townhouse museum near Kushida Shrine is a worthwhile free stop.
7:00 PM
Motsunabe dinner ~¥3,000–4,000/pp (~A$33–44)
Fukuoka's other iconic dish — offal (beef/pork tripe) hotpot with cabbage, chives, garlic in rich soy or miso broth, cooked at the table. Finish by adding rice or noodles to the leftover broth.

Takoyaki ~¥600 (8 pcs). Okonomiyaki ~¥1,200. Kushikatsu ~¥200–400/skewer. Yakiniku at Tsuruhashi ~¥2,500 all-in. Tenma izakayas — locals' choice north of the tourist belt. Avoid Kuromon Ichiba Market which has become a tourist trap — overpriced for what it is.
Japan · Days 17–18
Hakone
14 – 16 October · 2 nights · Train from Tokyo/Osaka via Odawara
2 nights Kaiseki dinner · Ryokan breakfast Onsen · Ropeway · Lake Ashi · Owakudani
Getting to Hakone by train: From Osaka, take the Shinkansen to Odawara (~2h 30min, ¥13,870/pp), then Hakone Tozan Train to Hakone-Yumoto (15 min). Or take the Romancecar limited express from Shinjuku (80 min, ¥2,470 + ¥1,200 surcharge). Buy the Hakone Free Pass (¥7,100 2-day from Odawara / ~¥6,000 without Tokyo return) — covers all local transport: Tozan Train, cable car, ropeway, Lake Ashi cruise, and sightseeing buses. Buy digitally on the Odakyu app before departure.

Wednesday 14 October
Travel to Hakone — ryokan check-in, onsen evening
›
Morning
Shinkansen Osaka → Odawara → Hakone-Yumoto ~¥14,000–15,000/pp (~A$155–165) total
Shinkansen (Kodama) to Odawara, then Hakone Tozan Train to Hakone-Yumoto (15 min). From here, Tozan Train continues up the mountain to Gora with spectacular switchbacks through forest valleys.
Afternoon
Hakone Free Pass — activate on arrival ¥7,100/pp 2-day (~A$79) · Buy digitally in advance
The pass covers: Tozan railway, cable car up to Sounzan, Hakone Ropeway (one of Japan's most dramatic — volcanic valley views), Lake Ashi sightseeing cruise (pirate ship), and all sightseeing buses. Also gives discounts at 70+ attractions including onsen and museums. Worth it especially for a 2-night stay.
Afternoon
Check in to ryokan ¥12,000–20,000/pp per night incl. dinner + breakfast
Most ryokans include kaiseki dinner — multi-course Japanese seasonal cuisine, beautifully presented, served at your table. Local mountain produce and Hakone specialties.
Evening
Outdoor onsen (rotemburo) Included with ryokan stay
The open-air communal onsen bath in the evening with mountain air is the defining Hakone experience. Soak, relax, look at the stars. The ryokan breakfast the next morning — rice, miso, grilled fish, pickles, tamago — is as good as dinner.
18
Thursday 15 October
Hakone Loop — ropeway, Owakudani, Lake Ashi cruise, Open-Air Museum
›
9:00 AM
Hakone Ropeway — Owakudani volcanic valley Covered by Free Pass
From Sounzan (take cable car from Gora), the ropeway glides over an active volcanic valley with steam vents and sulfurous craters. Buy kuro tamago (black eggs hard-boiled in volcanic springs, ~¥600 for 5) at Owakudani Station — rumoured to add 7 years to your life.
11:30 AM
Lake Ashi pirate ship cruise + Hakone Shrine Cruise covered by Free Pass · Shrine free
The lake cruise gives the classic Fuji-over-water view (cloud permitting). Walk from Hakone-machi Port to Hakone Shrine — red torii gate standing in the lake is one of Japan's great sights. Very peaceful on an autumn weekday.
2:00 PM
Hakone Open-Air Museum ¥1,600/pp (~A$17.60) · Discounted with Free Pass to ¥1,400
One of Japan's best museums — sculptures and art installations spread across beautifully landscaped grounds with mountain views. A Picasso pavilion, glass mosaics, and a famous Foot Onsen where you can soak your feet in a hot spring inside the museum. Allow 2 hours.
Evening
Second night at ryokan — onsen again + rest
You've fully earned it. The outdoor rotemburo at night after a full day is the trip highlight for many people. Last onsen morning before returning to Tokyo.

Money tips
Best card for overseas spending: ubank (Australian bank, no foreign currency fee, no ATM withdrawal fee — use this for everything in Japan and Korea). Alternatively, a Wise multi-currency card for excellent exchange rates.

Cash: Japan is still heavily cash-based — carry ¥20,000–30,000 at all times. Withdraw from 7-Eleven Japan ATMs (accepts all foreign cards including ubank and Wise). Korea ATMs at 7-Eleven and Citibank also work.

JR Pass: Not worth it for this itinerary. Individual tickets for the short shinkansen hops are cheaper than the pass. The one exception worth considering is the Hakone Free Pass.

TeamLab Planets (Tokyo) — 30 September
Book tickets at teamlab.art immediately — walk-in almost never available. Select the 30 September timeslot. ¥3,200/pp (~A$35). One of the most-visited attractions in Tokyo so book earliest to get ideal time.

♨ Onsen etiquette reminders
For Hakone onsen: You bathe nude in traditional communal onsen (gender-separated). Shower thoroughly before entering the baths. No swimwear. If anyone has visible tattoos, the ryokan communal bath may not allow entry — ask when booking and consider private baths (kashikiri) which most ryokans offer for groups. The ryokan will provide yukata (cotton robe), towel, and amenities.
